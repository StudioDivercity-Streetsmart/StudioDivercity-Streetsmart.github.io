Why StreetSmart?

Streets were designed for cars. We think they should be designed for you.

Streets used to be where life happened — where kids ran out the front door, neighbours leaned over fences, and weeds found their way through the cracks. Somewhere along the way, we handed all of that over to cars.

StreetSmart is here to take it back. We help communities see their streets clearly, and reshape them into places where children roam freely, nature finds room to breathe, and neighbours actually stop to talk.

We built a platform. But what we're really building is a movement — for people, for nature, for the kind of belonging that starts right outside your door.

Myth-busters

What we got wrong about streets — and why it matters.

01 The myth

“We're not Amsterdam — we just don't have a walking culture.”

The truth

People always respond to the world we build for them. When streets welcome humans, humans show up. Infrastructure shapes behaviour, not the other way round.

02 The myth

“More road lanes will fix our traffic.”

The truth

They actually make it worse. It's called induced demand: build more lanes, attract more cars, refill the jam. Every. Single. Time.

03 The myth

“Pedestrian bridges are for pedestrians.”

The truth

They're really for cars. They send people up stairs and around detours so traffic never has to slow. Convenient for drivers, exhausting for everyone else.

04 The myth

“Bigger cars are safer.”

The truth

Safer for the people inside, riskier for everyone outside. Big SUVs and trucks take longer to stop and have huge blind spots — especially around kids.
